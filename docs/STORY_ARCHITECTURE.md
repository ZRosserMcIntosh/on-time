# The Rushlight — Story Architecture

> *"A small light is still a light."*
> For Yen, Rush, and Lu. — YenLu Games

---

## The Core Truth

This game is not about death.
It is about **unfinished love**.

Drew doesn't die mid-war. Drew dies right after saying something hopeful.
Right after making a plan for tomorrow.

You take tomorrow away.

That's the punch.

---

## The Five Arcs (100 Chapters)

| Arc | Chapters | Theme | Emotional Core |
|-----|----------|-------|----------------|
| **I — The Golden Days** | Prologue – 10 | Safety, wonder, becoming siblings | "This is what we have." |
| **II — The Cracks** | 11 – 19 | Things aren't what they seem, trust frays | "Something is wrong." |
| **III — The Fall** | 20 – 25 | Drew's death, the world breaks | "I wasn't allowed to finish loving." |
| **IV — The Dark** | 26 – 60 | Grief, fracture, wandering, rebuilding | "How do you go on when the light goes out?" |
| **V — The Rushlight** | 61 – 100 | Finding meaning, carrying what was lost forward | "A small light is still a light." |

## Arc I — The Golden Days (Prologue – Chapter 10)

### Purpose
Make the player **love Drew**. Not respect. Not admire. *Love.*

Every chapter must deposit something into the emotional bank account.
Small moments. Quiet scenes. Drew being a good older brother.
Not a hero. A person.

### Chapter Breakdown

**Prologue: "The Last Good Day"** ✅ (WRITTEN)
- Golden hour backyard. Planning a campout.
- Drew's "promise me you'll stick together" speech.
- Kimiru appears for the first time. "I've been waiting... for it to begin."
- SETS: promised_drew flag, initial relationships, first memory.

**Chapter 1: "The Fort"**
- The four siblings build a blanket fort in the woods behind the house.
- Drew teaches Rush how to tie a proper knot. Patient. Never condescending.
- Lu finds a weird symbol carved into a tree. Nobody thinks much of it.
- Yen gets scared of a noise. Drew carries her on his shoulders.
- Kimiru watches from a branch. Says nothing. Just watches.
- CHOICE: Rush can ask Drew "Are you ever scared?" → Drew's answer changes based on this.
- DEPOSITS: Drew's patience. Drew's physical safety. Drew as protector.

**Chapter 2: "The Storm"**
- A thunderstorm knocks out the power.
- Drew makes it an adventure — flashlight tag, shadow puppets.
- He tells a story about "the four lanterns" — foreshadowing the game's mythology.
- Rush and Lu argue over something small. Drew mediates without taking sides.
- Yen falls asleep in Drew's lap.
- Kimiru appears at the window during lightning. Eyes glow.
- CHOICE: Who does Rush sit next to during the story? → Relationship shift.
- DEPOSITS: Drew turns fear into wonder. Drew as storyteller. Drew as peacemaker.

**Chapter 3: "The Market"**
- The family goes to a weekend market in a strange town.
- Drew gives Rush money to buy something for Lu (or keeps it — player choice).
- They find an old woman selling lanterns. She looks at Drew too long.
- Drew buys a small rushlight — a medieval candle made from animal fat.
- "They used these when they couldn't afford real candles. A small light. But it was enough."
- The old woman whispers to Kimiru. Players can't hear what she says.
- CHOICE: Does Rush buy something for Lu, something for himself, or save the money?
- DEPOSITS: Drew's generosity. The rushlight as symbol. The world is slightly strange.

**Chapter 4: "The Lesson"**
- Drew is teaching Rush to ride a bike / swim / climb — something physical.
- He's patient when Rush fails. "Again. You've almost got it."
- Lu joins and is immediately better at it. Rush can get jealous or cheer her on.
- Drew notices Rush's frustration and pulls him aside.
- "Being good at something doesn't make you good. Being kind when someone's better — that does."
- Kimiru is sitting nearby. After Drew's line, Kimiru says: "He's right, you know."
- First time Kimiru has endorsed a human's words.
- CHOICE: How does Rush respond to Lu being better? → Virtue/vice adjustment.
- DEPOSITS: Drew's wisdom. Drew sees Rush — really sees him. Drew's philosophy.

**Chapter 5: "The Secret"**
- Drew tells Rush a secret — something vulnerable.
- Maybe he's scared about growing up. Maybe he misses someone.
- Maybe he doesn't know what he wants to be.
- This is Drew being human, not heroic. A kid admitting he's uncertain.
- Rush can respond with comfort, deflection, or share a secret back.
- Kimiru is present but turns away. Gives them privacy.
- CHOICE: Does Rush share his own fear, or change the subject?
- DEPOSITS: Drew's vulnerability. Mutual trust. The bond deepens beyond protector/protected.

**Chapter 6: "The Promise"**
- Something small goes wrong — a neighbor's meanness, a broken thing, an unfair rule.
- Drew gets angry. Not violent. Just... tight-jawed. Controlled.
- The kids see Drew be upset for the first time.
- He calms himself. Explains: "Getting mad is easy. Choosing what to do with it — that's the work."
- He makes a promise to the siblings about something specific and future-tense.
- A plan. A trip. A thing they'll do together "when summer comes."
- CHOICE: Rush can ask Drew to promise for real, or say "you don't have to promise."
- DEPOSITS: Drew has emotions. Drew controls them. Drew makes promises about the future.
- **THIS PROMISE WILL NEVER BE KEPT.**

**Chapter 7: "The Game"**
- A big group game — hide and seek, capture the flag, something physical and joyful.
- Drew is team captain. Picks Rush first (not because he's best — because he's brother).
- Lu strategizes. Yen cheats adorably. Drew pretends not to notice.
- Pure joy. Laughter. Running. The kind of afternoon you remember forever.
- Kimiru participates — acts as "referee" with dry commentary.
- No major choice. Just happiness.
- DEPOSITS: Joy. The memory of Drew laughing. The sound of it. Store this.

**Chapter 8: "The Photograph"**
- Someone takes a photo of the four siblings together.
- Drew insists everyone's in it. Positions them. Fixes Yen's hair.
- "Someday we'll look at this and remember exactly how this felt."
- Small tension: there's a moment where something feels fragile.
- Kimiru refuses to be in the photo. "Cats don't photograph well. Trust me."
- CHOICE: Rush can say "I want to remember this forever" or stay quiet.
- DEPOSITS: A frozen moment. The photo will become the most painful object in the game.

**Chapter 9: "The Night Watch"**
- Camping. Stars. Drew and Rush stay up after the others sleep.
- Drew points out constellations. Makes up names for the ones he doesn't know.
- A long, quiet conversation. Brothers.
- Drew says something like: "Whatever happens, you look after them. You're stronger than you think."
- Rush can promise or say "you'll always be here."
- Kimiru is awake. Listening. His eyes are sad.
- CHOICE: "I promise" vs "You'll always be here to do it yourself."
- DEPOSITS: The sacred conversation. The passing of responsibility Rush doesn't understand yet.
- **Drew's line is the setup. The death is the payoff.**

**Chapter 10: "Tomorrow"**
- The day before everything changes.
- An ordinary morning. Breakfast. Bickering. Drew making plans.
- Drew says: "Tomorrow we should—"
- And then something interrupts. Something small. A phone call. A knock.
- The chapter ends on Drew smiling.
- Kimiru, alone in the last frame: "...I'm sorry."
- No choice. No gameplay. Just a moment.
- DEPOSITS: Tomorrow. The plan that will never happen. Drew's last smile.

## Arc II — The Cracks (Chapters 11–19)

### Purpose
Unease. The world shifts. Trust frays. Something is hunting them.

Ghost appears — a figure in the periphery. Never fully seen.
Shepherd appears — a trusted figure. Helpful. Kind. Wrong.

The kids don't know who to trust. Drew does. And that's what kills him.

### Key Beats

- **Ch 11-13**: Strange things happen. The carved symbol returns. Kimiru becomes evasive.
- **Ch 14-15**: Ghost is spotted. Drew tells the kids to stay close. Drew is worried but hiding it.
- **Ch 16**: Shepherd arrives. Warm. Protective. Offers help. Everyone trusts him. Even Drew — almost.
- **Ch 17**: Drew confides in Rush: "Something about him doesn't sit right." Player choice to agree or dismiss.
- **Ch 18**: Shepherd does something genuinely kind. Saves someone. Proves himself. Drew relaxes.
- **Ch 19**: "The Eve" — Drew is hopeful. The threat seems handled. Shepherd is on their side. Tomorrow is safe.
  - Drew makes a plan for tomorrow.
  - Drew laughs.
  - Drew is at his most alive.
  - **This is the peak. The highest point before the fall.**

## Arc III — The Fall (Chapters 20–25)

### Chapter 20: "The Ambush"

Ghost attacks. Chaos. Drew fights — not with weapons. With his body. Shielding.
Shepherd is there.
Shepherd helps.
The kids are safe.
Drew is hurt but standing.
They won.

Hope.

### Chapter 21: "The Betrayal"

Shepherd turns.

Not dramatically. Quietly.

A door that should be open is locked.
A path that should be safe leads nowhere.
Something Shepherd said doesn't match.

Drew realizes first.

Drew says: "Take the kids. Go."

Rush: "What about you?"

Drew: "I said go."

### Chapter 22: "The Fall of Drew"

Drew confronts Shepherd.
Not to fight. To buy time.

Shepherd doesn't monologue.
Shepherd says something small and cold:
"This was always going to happen."

Ghost appears.
Drew is between Ghost and the kids.
Drew doesn't move.

Impact.

Fast.

No dramatic music.
No slow motion.

Just—

Someone says: "Drew?"

Silence.

"Drew?"

Shepherd, quietly: "Get up."

Then louder.

Then—

"No."

Again, breaking:

"No, no, no, no."

Not a speech.
Just repetition.

Kimiru is there.
He opens his mouth.
Stops.

Says: **"I do not understand this."**

That's the line.

Drew's hand reaches for Rush.
Not dramatically.
Just... reaching.

"Tell them... I was going to..."

He doesn't finish.

He never finishes.

**The lantern goes out.**

### Chapter 23: "Silence"

No gameplay.
No choices.
Just the world, quiet.
The siblings, sitting.
Kimiru, for the first time, curled small.
The photo from Chapter 8 appears.

A long, empty scene.

The player can only press one button.
It does nothing.

### Chapter 24: "The Sound"

Shepherd is gone.
The kids are somewhere safe. Or at least still.

Rush is alone.

And the player hears what grief sounds like:

Not crying. Not screaming.

Just Rush saying: "He was going to—"

And stopping.

Starting: "We were supposed to—"

Stopping.

Starting: "Tomorrow—"

Stopping.

Every unfinished sentence is a callback to a promise Drew made.
The summer trip. The constellation names. The thing they'd do tomorrow.

All interrupted.

All permanent.

CHOICE: Rush can punch a wall, hold Yen, or sit in silence.
Each one is valid. None of them fix it.

### Chapter 25: "The Rushlight"

The aftermath.

Lu finds Drew's rushlight — the one from the market.

She lights it.

It's small.
It flickers.
It barely lights the room.

But it's there.

Kimiru looks at it.

"...A small light is still a light."

The title screen phrase.
Now you know what it means.
Now it hurts.

## Arc IV — The Dark (Chapters 26–60)

### Purpose
Living with loss. The siblings fracture and slowly, painfully, rebuild.

### Structure
- **26-35**: Immediate grief. Anger. Blame. Rush blames himself. Lu withdraws. Yen doesn't understand.
- **36-45**: The quest continues but hollow. Mechanical. Going through motions.
- **46-55**: Small moments of reconnection. Rush remembers Drew's words. Starts applying them.
- **56-60**: The siblings face a crisis together. Without Drew. And they don't fall apart.

### Kimiru's Role
- Chapters 26-30: Nearly silent. Grief-stricken. Breaks his own rules.
- Chapters 31-40: Slowly returns. More gentle than before. Less cryptic.
- Chapters 41-50: Begins to guide again, but differently. More honest.
- Chapters 51-60: "I could not save him. I can still walk with you."

### Shepherd
- Returns periodically. Not as villain. As consequence.
- He believes he was right. That's worse than evil.
- His justification is cold and logical.
- He is never defeated with violence. He is defeated with *the thing Drew taught them*.

## Arc V — The Rushlight (Chapters 61–100)

### Purpose
The light doesn't replace what was lost. It carries it forward.

### Structure
- **61-75**: The siblings find their own strengths. Each one carries a piece of Drew.
- **76-90**: The final confrontation. Not with Shepherd. With the choice: become bitter, or become kind.
- **91-99**: Resolution. Healing is not forgetting. Scars are not weakness.
- **100**: "The Last Good Night" — mirrors the prologue. The siblings, older. A campfire. The rushlight.
  - Rush tells the story of Drew to someone new.
  - Not perfectly. Not without pain.
  - But he tells it.
  - Kimiru, old now, sitting by the fire: "That's how it happened. More or less."
  - Credits roll over the photo from Chapter 8.

## The Death Scene Rules

### What We Do NOT Do:
- ❌ Blood or graphic injury
- ❌ Drew screaming in pain
- ❌ Slow-motion dramatic death poses
- ❌ Villain monologue during death
- ❌ Anyone saying "it's going to be okay"
- ❌ Drew delivering a perfect final speech

### What We DO:
- ✅ Drew reaching — not dramatically, just reaching
- ✅ An unfinished sentence
- ✅ Silence where there should be sound
- ✅ Kimiru failing to speak
- ✅ The strong one (Shepherd/Drew) breaking
- ✅ Callbacks to every promise, every plan, every "tomorrow"
- ✅ A button that does nothing (Chapter 23)
- ✅ The photo

### The Four Beats:
1. **Impact** — fast, almost missed
2. **Confusion** — "Drew?"
3. **Denial** — "No, no, no, no"
4. **Collapse** — Kimiru: "I do not understand this."

## Kimiru's Arc

| Phase | Chapters | Behavior |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Observer | Prologue–10 | Watches. Cryptic. Tests. |
| Warning | 11–19 | Evasive. Worried. Trying to prepare without saying why. |
| Breaking | 20–25 | Fails. Goes silent. "I do not understand this." |
| Grief | 26–35 | Small. Quiet. Curls up. Doesn't guide. |
| Return | 36–50 | Gentle. Less cryptic. More honest. |
| Companion | 51–100 | No longer a guide above. A friend beside. |

### Kimiru's Defining Lines:
- Prologue: "I've been waiting... for it to begin."
- Chapter 22: "I do not understand this."
- Chapter 25: "A small light is still a light."
- Chapter 50: "I could not save him. I can still walk with you."
- Chapter 100: "That's how it happened. More or less."

## The Emotional Bank Account

Every chapter before Drew's death makes a deposit.
The death scene withdraws everything at once.

| Chapter | Deposit |
|---------|---------|
| Prologue | Drew's "stick together" promise |
| 1 | Drew's patience |
| 2 | Drew turns fear into wonder |
| 3 | Drew's generosity, the rushlight |
| 4 | Drew really *sees* Rush |
| 5 | Drew's vulnerability |
| 6 | Drew's anger control + **the unkept promise** |
| 7 | Drew's laugh |
| 8 | **The photograph** |
| 9 | The sacred night conversation |
| 10 | "Tomorrow" — **Drew's last smile** |

Every one of these comes back in Chapter 24 as an unfinished sentence.

## Character Archetypes (Player-Driven)

Based on choices, Rush becomes:

- **Steadfast**: High virtues, low vices, managed grief → Drew's true heir
- **Scarred but Good**: High virtues, moderate grief → Carrying it, but carrying it well
- **Hardened**: Moderate virtues, high vices → Survived, but at cost
- **Broken**: Low virtues, high grief → Lost himself in the loss
- **Human**: Mixed everything → The most realistic. Imperfect. Still trying.

The ending adapts. Not "good/bad." Just *different kinds of survival.*

## Technical Notes

### Callback System
The engine tracks every promise, plan, and "tomorrow" moment.
Chapter 24 dynamically generates unfinished sentences from what Drew actually said,
based on which chapters the player experienced most deeply.

### The Silent Button (Chapter 23)
A single input prompt appears: `[SPACE]`
It does nothing.
After 30 seconds, the scene advances on its own.
The player learns they cannot fix this.

### The Photo
First appears in Chapter 8.
Returns in Chapter 23 (silence), Chapter 25 (rushlight), Chapter 100 (credits).
Each time it means something different.

### Music
- Chapters 1–10: Warm acoustic. Gentle.
- Chapter 22: **No music.** Just ambient sound fading to silence.
- Chapter 23: A single sustained note. Then nothing.
- Chapter 24: Drew's theme, played wrong. Missing notes. Broken.
- Chapter 25: Drew's theme fragment + new theme. Small. A rushlight.
